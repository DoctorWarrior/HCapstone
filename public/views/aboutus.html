About Gallaudet

Gallaudet University

Gallaudet University is the world leader in liberal education and career development for deaf and hard of hearing students. The University enjoys an international reputation for its outstanding programs and for the quality of the research it conducts on the history, language, culture, and other topics related to deaf people.

About the Honors Program

The Honors Program at Gallaudet University

The Honors Program is a two-tier program offering students advanced preparation for graduate school or professional employment. We offer General Studies Honors (where students work towards Honors completing their General Studies Requirements) and University Honors (where students work towards an Honors degree during their last two years and complete an Honors Capstone). Students who graduate with University Honors will be eminently prepared for admission to top-tier graduate or professional programs.
To aid students in their quest for excellence, we provide a number of benefits including dedicated study facilities, scholarships, social programs, and enhanced academic preparation.

If you have questions about our program, please contact honors@gallaudet.edu.

About Our UI Designer

Izumi Takizawa

Izumi Takizawa is from Japan and currently in the International Development Program in M.A. at Gallaudet University. Previously she earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Faculty of Industrial Technology in the Department of Synthetic Design at The National University Corporation of Tsukuba University of Technology (NTUT). Izumi has sold her artwork with a Deaf group at Design Festa in Tokyo. She made a children's book specifically for the Deaf, and signed the story at a school for the Deaf in Japan.

Izumi's dream is to make children's books for Deaf children and their parents, in order to strengthen their bond and teach the parents sign language so they can communicate effectively with their child. She has strong illustration and graphic design skills, and is currently working as an artist in the National Science Foundation's Science of Learning Center on Visual Language and Visual Learning (VL2) at Gallaudet University.
